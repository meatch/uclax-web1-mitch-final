What is the difference between jpg, gif, png and SVG images?

  • JPEG: Millions of Colors (Photography), along with proprietary Lossy Compression
  • GIF: 8-bit Format, Gif Animations (Can get large),
  • PNG: Supports Alpha transparency (32-bit). Best parts fo Jpeg and Gif, Lossless Compression, Support 8-bit and Millions of Colors. Tends be larger in size. (Embedded text for SEO which may be benificial)
  • SVG: Scaleable Vector Graphics. Vectors can scale without loss of degradation. Lossless. Animation and Transparency.

What is considered the right size for an image or video asset?

How do we make the file as small as possible without losing too much quality?

  • Keeping file size small for loading
  • Static images = Min size of destination of image.
  • 2560 or 2500 Pixels for full width images, smaller views increment down by 500 pixels
  • 1800 to 2000, thumbs = 1500 in lightboxes
  • Videos: Less than 2MB total.
  • Scaling above 100% will pixelate the image (bitmap/raster)
  • Mobile/Device will resize the image down
  • Use low rez images. Copyright concerns.
  • Twice the size for pixel depth
  • 720 and 1080 for Video
